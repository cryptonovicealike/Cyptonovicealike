Bitcoin vs. Ethereum: What's the Actual Difference?

5 min read 🎧 Recommended Podcast: Bankless

Think of Bitcoin as digital gold. It was designed to be a store of value, a way to transfer wealth without banks. Ethereum, on the other hand, is like a smartphone operating system – a platform where developers build applications (dApps) that run on blockchain.

Bitcoin: The Digital Gold

Bitcoin has a fixed supply of 21 million coins. It's simple: send, receive, hold. It's not designed to do much else. That simplicity makes it incredibly secure and reliable for storing value.

Ethereum: The World Computer

Ethereum introduced smart contracts – self‑executing contracts with the terms directly written into code. This opened the door for DeFi (decentralized finance), NFTs, and thousands of other applications.

Key Takeaways for Beginners

  • Bitcoin = Store of value (like gold)
  • Ethereum = Platform for applications (like the internet)
  • Most people start with Bitcoin, then explore Ethereum's ecosystem
