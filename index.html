Why Bitcoin? The Full Picture

From decentralization to institutional adoption — learn why Bitcoin is considered digital gold and more…

7 min read

Why invest in Bitcoin and other cryptocurrencies

Bitcoin is an alternative form of money and a payment system. It is decentralized. No single company or central bank controls it. The network is maintained by people around the world who use it and verify transactions.

Bitcoin works as both a currency and a network. You can send value directly to someone else without a third party. Transactions can settle within minutes. For example, on April 12, 2020, someone transferred 161,500 BTC, worth over $1.1 billion at the time, in a single transaction. It settled in about 10 minutes with a fee of around $0.63.

Security and decentralization

Bitcoin has remained secure because of its distributed network. Thousands of computers across the world verify transactions. This makes it very hard to manipulate.

To attack the network, someone would need to control most of its computing power. This is known as a 51% attack. The cost and resources required make this highly impractical.

Today, tens of thousands of nodes verify transactions independently. This decentralization is one of Bitcoin’s strongest advantages.

Bitcoin vs fiat currency

Traditional currencies lose value over time due to inflation. Governments can print more money, which reduces purchasing power.

Bitcoin is different. It has a fixed supply. Only 21 million coins will ever exist. New supply is introduced at a decreasing rate through an event called the halving, which happens roughly every four years.

This limited supply creates scarcity. As demand grows, the price tends to rise over time. This is why many people see Bitcoin as a store of value, similar to gold.

Key qualities of money

You argue that Bitcoin meets strong qualities of money:

  • Portability: You can send large amounts anywhere in the world quickly.
  • Durability: It exists digitally and cannot degrade.
  • Scarcity: Supply is fixed and predictable.
  • Acceptability: It is accepted globally, with thousands of ATMs and businesses supporting it.
  • Divisibility: It can be split into very small units.

Institutional adoption

The cryptocurrency market has grown rapidly and has attracted both retail and institutional investors.

Major financial players have entered the space:

  • Fidelity Investments offers crypto services
  • CME Group provides Bitcoin futures
  • JPMorgan Chase and BBVA have developed crypto-related products

Large institutions and universities have also allocated capital to crypto:

  • Harvard University
  • Yale University
  • Stanford University

Investors like Paul Tudor Jones have publicly supported Bitcoin as a hedge against inflation.

Regulation and market access

Before regulation, crypto markets were less accessible to large institutions. Many companies needed legal clarity before participating.

Regulation allowed institutions to enter the market. This increased capital inflow and legitimacy.

Blockchain transparency

Bitcoin runs on blockchain technology. This is a public ledger that records every transaction.

  • Anyone can view transactions
  • No single party controls the system
  • Records are permanent and transparent

This reduces fraud and increases trust in the system.

Criticism and resistance

Some financial institutions and central banks have opposed Bitcoin. Blockchain challenges traditional systems by removing intermediaries and increasing transparency.

There have also been scams in the crypto space. These have slowed adoption and created distrust among new users.
