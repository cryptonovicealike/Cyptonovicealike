Bitcoin vs. Ethereum: What's the Actual Difference?

Bitcoin and Ethereum are often mentioned together, but they serve completely different purposes. Think of Bitcoin as digital gold – a store of value meant to be scarce and secure. Ethereum, on the other hand, is like a global computer where developers can build decentralized applications (dApps).

Key Differences:

  • Purpose: Bitcoin is a peer-to-peer electronic cash system; Ethereum is a platform for smart contracts and dApps.
  • Supply: Bitcoin has a capped supply of 21 million; Ethereum has no fixed supply but has mechanisms to reduce inflation.
  • Consensus: Both now use Proof-of-Stake (Ethereum switched in 2022), but Bitcoin still uses Proof-of-Work.
  • Programming: Ethereum’s native language Solidity allows complex applications; Bitcoin’s script is limited.

In simple terms: Bitcoin is a currency; Ethereum is an ecosystem. Both are revolutionary, but understanding their roles helps you navigate the crypto space with clarity.
